Insights from Superstore Sales Data Analysis

1. Category Performance
Technology has the highest total sales (836K) and best average profit per item (78.75).
Furniture has very low average profit (8.70) despite decent sales volume.
Office Supplies sells the most units (22,906) but at lower margins.

2. Regional Performance
West region leads in both sales (764K) and profit (98K).
South region is the weakest with only 30K in total profit.
Central and East are mid performers.

3. Top Products
Canon imageCLASS 2200 Copier is the best selling product with 61,599 in total sales.
Most top products are from Technology category.

4. Top Customers
Sean Miller is the highest spending customer at 25,043 total.
Most top customers are from Consumer segment.

5. Monthly and Yearly Trends
Sales peak in September every year, likely due to Q3 buying cycles.
February is consistently the slowest month.
Revenue has grown from 484K in 2014 to 733K in 2017, around 51% growth overall.

6. Discount Impact
Orders with no discount have the highest average profit (66.90 per item).
Discounts above 20% start generating losses on average.
High discounts above 40% result in average loss of 106 per item which is a major issue.

7. Loss Making Products
Cubify CubeX 3D Printers and Cisco TelePresence units are the biggest loss makers.
These are high priced items being sold at heavy discounts which wipes out profit.

8. Customer Segments
Consumer segment dominates with 2586 orders and 1.16M in sales.
Home Office is the smallest segment but has a decent profit to order ratio.

9. Data Quality
No major duplicate issues found. Some customers have 2 orders on the same date
but these appear to be split shipments, not actual duplicates.
